Course project technical report, University of California, Santa Barbara · 2025

A Simulation Study Comparing the Robustness of ANOVA and Permutation Tests Under Assumption Violations

Course project technical report, University of California, Santa Barbara · 2024

Compares Type I error and power for ANOVA and permutation tests across different distributions and variance structures, providing guidance on when non-parametric approaches are most useful.

Research interests

Areas I’m excited to keep working on in graduate school and beyond.

Broadly, I’m interested in how data science and econometrics can inform:

  • Health equity and access to care, especially through large administrative and survey datasets.
  • Public policy evaluation using causal inference and quasi-experimental methods.
  • Spatial and temporal modeling of risk, including county-level and neighborhood-level analyses.
  • Transparent, interpretable machine learning models that can be used by policymakers and practitioners.
