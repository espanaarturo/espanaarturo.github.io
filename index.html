Arturo Espana

Statistics & Data Science · Economics · UC Santa Barbara

I’m a senior at UC Santa Barbara double majoring in Statistics & Data Science and Economics (graduating June 2026). I build reproducible pipelines and interpretable models for health, policy, and spatial data.

My recent work includes county-level modeling of preventable hospitalizations using Random Forests and Negative Binomial regression, as well as data engineering and time-series analysis for Ghana’s petroleum market research.

I’m especially interested in health equity, causal inference, and practical ML that can support decision-making in public systems.
